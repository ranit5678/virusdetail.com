Types of computer viruses

Computer virus can be categorised in different ways. Here, we shall dicsuss the program virus, boot sector virus, macro virus and Trojan horse.

How many types of virus are there?

  • program virus
    • Program viruses can travel on CD, or across the internet through e-mail attachments.They infect executable program files, such as those with extensions like .BIN, .COM, .EXE ,.OVL, .DRV(driver) and .SYS(device driver). Examples:Sunday and Cascade

  • Boot sector virus
    • The boot sector virus hides in the boot sector of the hard disk.The boot sector is the first part of hard disk that contains data used to boot or start a computer system.
    Examples:Polyboot.B and AntiExe

  • Macro virus
    • The Macro virus spreads by infecting documents and spreadsheets such as words or excel file.
    Example - Mellisa or Bablas

  • Trojan Horse

    • Trojan horse is a program that looks harmless but introduces virus into your computer. The Trojan Horse program could be disguised as agame or an antivirus program but when executed, it can destroy the data stoted in your computer. The Trojan comes from the greek Mythology.


Spam,Adware,Malware and Spyware

Spam refers to the junk or Unwanted Emails. These Emails may carry advertisement or may be a way of spreading virus or worms.

To protect your inboxes from spam, take these precautions:

  • Do not give your e-mail adress to unknown people or companies
  • Use spam filters in your to block spam e-mails.
  • Delete spam mail without reading them.

  • Adware is a form of software that displays unwanted advertisement when the user is online.

  • Spyware can steal information from another computer.

  • Malware short for malicious software, includes computer viruses, worms,Trojan horse, spyware, dishonest adware, scareware or crimeware Program which are designed to damage a single computer or computer networks. Malware can slow down computer or entire network.

  • Worms are small pieces of software that use computer networks and security holes to replicate themselves.
